1. What Exactly is EXIF Metadata?

EXIF (Exchangeable Image File Format) metadata is non-visible data embedded in image files by cameras and smartphones. It includes:

  • Camera make & model, exposure details (ISO, aperture, shutter speed)
  • Image information: dimensions, resolution, color space, thumbnails
  • Date and time captured
  • GPS coordinates (if location services are enabled)
  • Software used for editing and sometimes author or copyright info

This metadata travels with the image, from your camera to your computer, to wherever you choose to upload it—oftentimes without you realizing it. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

3. How to Check EXIF Metadata in Your Images

  • Windows: Right-click → Properties → Details tab.
  • Mac: Right-click → Get Info, or open in Preview and use Tools → Inspector.
  • Mobile: Use built-in photo info or third-party EXIF viewer apps.
  • Online tools: Upload and instantly inspect metadata before sharing.
